3 Obsidian Plugins I Use in 2026

TS
By Tech Simpld
•
Available Now
•
8 Min Read

Out of the box, Obsidian is just a plain text editor. If you don't know how to customize it, your notes will quickly turn into a cluttered, unorganized mess that feels impossible to navigate.

But the real magic of Obsidian happens when you unlock community plugins. In today's guide, I'm going to show you a few of the plugins I use to completely transform Obsidian from a basic notepad into a fully automated, visual project management system — all in under 10 minutes. No complex code or steep learning curves. Just the essentials. Let’s get into it.

Step 1: The Kanban Plugin (Visual Project Management)

First up is the Kanban plugin. If you've ever used Trello, you already know how powerful this layout is. Once enabled, you can turn any plain note into a visual project board.

You can create lists for your tasks, type out your cards, and literally drag-and-drop your ideas across your custom workflow. It's the absolute best way to manage video scripts, content calendars, or daily habits inside Obsidian without leaving the app.

Step 2: Dataview (The Automated Search Engine)

Next is Dataview, which is widely considered the ultimate powerhouse plugin for Obsidian. Think of Dataview as a search engine that treats your entire vault like a private database.

Instead of manually linking or looking for your notes, a simple one-line query will automatically gather, sort, and display every single file matching a specific criteria—like listing every book review you wrote this month or every task that's still outstanding.

Step 3: Templater (Infinite Workflow Automation)

To tie this all together, you need Templater. This plugin completely automates new note creation by inserting dynamic variables like dates, times, and pre-set structures the exact second you open a file.

Pro-Tip: When you back up Templater with a Stream Deck shortcut, you can launch a perfectly formatted daily note, complete with your dynamic Dataview query and project trackers, with a single button press.
