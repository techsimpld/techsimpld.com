Stop Network Ads: Deploy Pi-hole on Proxmox LXC

TS
By Tech Simpld
•
Available Now
•
5 Min Read

Ads on the internet have gotten completely out of hand. They slow down your browsing, track your data, and turn basic web surfing into an absolute minefield.

Sure, you could install a browser extension like uBlock, but what about your smart TV? Your mobile apps? Your streaming devices? You can’t install traditional ad-blockers on those.

That’s where Pi-hole comes in. It’s a network-wide ad blocker that acts as a black hole for advertisements. It intercepts ad requests at the DNS level before they even download to your devices.

And today, we’re going to deploy it inside a lightweight Linux Container—or LXC—on Proxmox.

Why an LXC?

Why an LXC instead of a full virtual machine? Efficiency. Pi-hole requires almost zero processing power. By running it as an LXC container, it shares the host kernel, boots instantly, and uses less than 1 gigabyte of RAM, leaving your Proxmox resources free for your heavier self-hosting workloads. Let's look at how to get this set up in just a few steps.

Step 1: Open the Proxmox Shell

To get this running flawlessly, we are going to use a streamlined shortcut: the community helper scripts by tteck. First, log into your Proxmox web interface, click on your main node on the left sidebar, and open up the Shell console.

Step 2: Execute the Creator Script

Next, we need to run the automatic setup command. Paste this official Pi-hole script into your terminal and press enter. I’ve left this exact command below so you can easily copy and paste it: 

curl -sSL https://install.pi-hole.net | bash

Step 3: Container Configurations

The script wizard will pop up and ask if you want to create a new Pi-hole LXC. Hit Yes. For 99% of home networks, the default allocation settings are absolutely perfect. Follow the prompts using the default selections, and the script will automatically download a clean container template, allocate system resources, and pull down the latest Pi-hole framework.

Step 4: Save Your Login Credentials

Once the automation finishes building, the script will output your container's newly assigned IP Address and your generated Admin Password. Make sure you write these down or take a quick screenshot, because we need them right now.

Step 5: Route Your Traffic

Open up a new browser tab, type in that IP address followed by /admin, and log in using your password. Welcome to your new ad-blocking command center.

The final, crucial step to make this work for your entire household is to route your traffic through it. Log into your home router's settings gateway, navigate to your DHCP or LAN DNS configurations, and change your Primary DNS IP address to match the IP address of your new Pi-hole container.

Conclusion

Now, whenever any device on your network tries to load an application or webpage, your router points it to the Pi-hole. If it’s an ad or tracker, it gets blocked instantly. If it’s real content, it passes right through.

Your network is now faster, cleaner, and significantly more private—all running on a tiny fraction of your Proxmox server's footprint.
